# Outreach kit: drive leads to "Get free opportunities"

Everything here points to one place:

**https://www.phildave.com/free-opportunities**

That page asks for the prospect's trade, where they bid, and whether they are
new to bidding, then the lead lands in your inbox (win@phildave.com) and the
CRM. The whole point of this kit is to get people to that page. Keep the link
short in speech ("phildave.com slash free opportunities").

---

## 1. Cold call: the closing line

Use this when a call is going nowhere or they are not ready to book. It is the
soft, give-value-first ask that fits the whole model.

> "Tell you what. No pitch. Give me your trade and where you bid and I will
> send you a few real government opportunities you have not seen, free. If they
> are useful, we talk. If not, you have lost nothing. I will text you a link
> right now, takes two minutes."

Then text immediately, while you are still on the phone:

> Here is that link, takes 2 minutes and I will send you real opportunities in
> your trade: https://www.phildave.com/free-opportunities - Phil

Voicemail version:

> "Hi [name], Phil Dave. I find government bids worth chasing for [trade] shops.
> No pitch, I will just send you a few real opportunities in your area for free.
> I will text you a link. Two minutes and you will see what you have been
> missing."

## 2. Email signature blurb

Drop this under your normal signature so every email you send is a soft ask.

> ---
> Phil Dave - Government Opportunity Intelligence
> I find the government bids worth your time, in Canada and the US.
> See what you are missing, free: https://www.phildave.com/free-opportunities

Short version for replies:

> Want a few real opportunities in your trade, free? https://www.phildave.com/free-opportunities

## 3. Follow-up email (after a call or a drop-in)

Subject: A few opportunities for [company]

> what I do: tell me your trade and where you bid and I will send back a short
> list of real, current government opportunities that fit, already qualified,
> with links to the source bid. No cost, no obligation.
>
> Two minutes here: https://www.phildave.com/free-opportunities
>
> Phil

## 4. Door-to-door leave-behind (printable QR)

For the Thursday field route. Hand it over, or leave it at the desk. The QR
goes straight to the form.

- QR files are in this folder: `free-opportunities-qr.svg` (best for print,
  scales to any size) and `free-opportunities-qr.png`.
- Suggested card text to print around the QR:

> **See the government bids you are missing. Free.**
> Scan this. Tell me your trade and where you bid. I will send you a short list
> of real, current opportunities that fit, already qualified.
> No cost. No obligation. I reply within 1 to 2 business days.
>
> [ QR CODE ]
>
> Phil Dave - Government Opportunity Intelligence
> phildave.com/free-opportunities

To print: open the SVG in any browser or design tool, drop it onto a card or
flyer, and add the text above. The SVG stays sharp at any size, so it works on a
business card or a full sheet.

## 5. Where to place the link

- Email signature (above).
- Text after every cold call (above).
- LinkedIn: headline or featured link, and the first comment on any post.
- Google Business Profile and any directory listing.
- Print: business cards, flyers, the QR leave-behind.
- The website already pushes it: header button, homepage hero, and the
  bottom call-to-action on every page now lead here.

## Tracking what works

If you want to know which channel drives leads, add a tag to the link per
channel using a query string, for example:

- Cold-call text: `https://www.phildave.com/free-opportunities?src=call`
- Email signature: `https://www.phildave.com/free-opportunities?src=email`
- QR leave-behind: `https://www.phildave.com/free-opportunities?src=qr`

The page works the same with or without the tag. Capturing the `src` value as a
lead field is a small follow-up if you want it reported in the CRM.
